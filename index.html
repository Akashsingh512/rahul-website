Symptoms of Blood Cancer

Common symptoms include:

  • Persistent fever
  • Frequent infections
  • Unexplained fatigue and weakness
  • Unintentional weight loss
  • Night sweats
  • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • Bone and joint pain
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pale skin and anemia
  • Loss of appetite

If you or your loved one is experiencing these symptoms, timely consultation with a specialist is essential.

When Is Bone Marrow Transplant Needed?

Bone Marrow Transplant (BMT) is an advanced treatment option recommended for certain blood cancers and bone marrow disorders when conventional therapies alone may not provide the desired results.

Bone Marrow Transplant may be considered in:

  • Leukemia
  • Lymphoma

  • Aplastic Anemia
  • Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS)

Know More About Transplant Types

Understanding Bone Marrow Transplant Types

Autologous Bone Marrow Transplant

In an Autologous Transplant, the patient's own healthy stem cells are collected and preserved before high-dose chemotherapy. These stem cells are then infused back into the body to restore healthy blood cell production.

Commonly used in: Multiple Myeloma, Certain types of Lymphoma

Allogeneic Bone Marrow Transplant

An Allogeneic Transplant involves the use of stem cells from a compatible donor. The donor may be a sibling, parent, child, or an unrelated matched donor.

Frequently performed for: Leukemia, Aplastic Anemia, Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS), Certain blood cancers and bone marrow disorders.

Haploidentical Bone Marrow Transplant

Uses stem cells from a partially matched family member. This option provides hope for patients who do not have a fully matched donor.

Used in: Acute Leukemia, Aplastic Anemia, Bone Marrow Failure Disorders.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Blood Cancer Curable?
Many blood cancers are treatable, and some patients may achieve long-term remission or cure depending on the type of disease, stage at diagnosis, and response to treatment. Early diagnosis and timely treatment play an important role in improving outcomes.
What Is a Bone Marrow Transplant?
Bone Marrow Transplant, also known as Stem Cell Transplant, is a procedure used to replace damaged or diseased bone marrow with healthy stem cells.
Who Needs a Bone Marrow Transplant?
Bone Marrow Transplant may be recommended for patients with Leukemia, Lymphoma, Multiple Myeloma, Aplastic Anemia, Myelodysplastic Syndrome, and certain inherited blood disorders.
